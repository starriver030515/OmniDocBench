Both the valentamine ring and the chain length of acarbose contributed to its extremely high affinity for the active site of GA. The role of a basic nitrogen in the formation of tight complexes with substrate 1 of GA is demonstrated by the binding of both acarbose (1) and 1-decoylonitrilevin (5), a structural analogue of D-glucose much preferred by GA over the planar inhibitor and transition-state ana-logue of -glucone-1,5-lactone. Finally, the formation of ternary complexes of GA containing either cyclodextrin and acarbose, or the L-lodo derivative and acarbose, indicates two different binding sites, one in the catalytic domain and the second in the starch-binding domain. Co-ordinated binding of ligands at these sites suggests that interactions of the two sites may be important in the hydrolysis of certain long-chain substrates.
